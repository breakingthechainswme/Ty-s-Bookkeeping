Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

The most common questions small business owners ask about bookkeeping services.

How much does monthly bookkeeping cost?
Pricing depends on your transaction volume and complexity. Most small businesses fall in a predictable monthly range. Schedule a free call and Ty will give you a straightforward quote — no surprises.
Do I need QuickBooks already set up?
Not at all. Ty can set up QuickBooks from scratch, clean up an existing file, or work with what you already have. He'll assess what makes the most sense for your business.
How far behind can my books be?
Catch-up bookkeeping is one of the most common services. Whether you're a few months or a couple years behind, Ty has handled it before and will get you current and organized.
What information do I need to provide?
Typically: bank statements, credit card statements, and access to any receipts or invoices. Most clients connect their bank accounts directly to QuickBooks, which makes the process much smoother.
How do you communicate with clients?
Ty keeps communication simple — phone, email, or text. You'll get monthly reports and he's available when you have questions. No complicated portals or confusing systems.
Can you help me understand my financial reports?
Absolutely. Part of the job is making sure your reports actually make sense to you. Ty walks through your numbers in plain language so you can make informed decisions.
Do you work with my CPA or tax preparer?
Yes — and your CPA will love you for having clean books. Ty can coordinate directly with your tax professional and make sure they have everything they need at tax time.
How long does setup or onboarding take?
Most clients are up and running within 1–2 weeks. The first month includes a thorough review of your existing records and getting everything organized before ongoing service begins.
Is this only for businesses in St. George?
Ty primarily serves Southern Utah — St. George, Washington, Hurricane, Cedar City, and surrounding areas — but remote clients are welcome too. Everything can be handled digitally.
What's the difference between a bookkeeper and a CPA?
A bookkeeper keeps your records clean and current throughout the year. A CPA handles tax strategy and filing. You need both — and having an organized bookkeeper makes your CPA's job (and your bill) much smaller.
